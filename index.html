Research

My research focuses on the development of novel methods for estimating uncertainty in geophysical modelling systems. Such uncertainty may comefrom the chaotic nature of the Earth system, from incomplete understanding of the relevant physics, or from shortcomings of the observations we feed into our models. Ensuring that these uncertainties are well-specified is critical for understanding how confident scientists can be in model forecasts. Additionally, it provides insight into a range of possible forecast outcomes, which is especially important for the prediction of extreme events that have significant socioeconomic implications (severe storms, heat waves, etc.).

Effective quantification of all of the different sources of uncertainty in atmospheric models is, however, incredibly challenging, owing to the high dimensionality and nonlinearity of Earth system models. My research involves collaboration with meteorologists, modellers, statisticians, and data assimilation scientists, working to design practical uncertainty quantification methods that respect these computational and theoretical constraints.
